# Walker's Healthcare Power BI Dashboard Report

## Executive Summary

This Healthcare Analytics project provides a comprehensive examination of patient financial data, treatment patterns, and operational efficiency across multiple healthcare facilities.The dataset captures patient-level data spanning hospital visits, diagnoses, departmental billing, insurance coverage, medication costs, room charges, and patient satisfaction scores across cities in England, Northern Ireland, Scotland, and Wales.

The primary objective of this project is to transform raw healthcare records into actionable insights that support operational, financial, and clinical decision-making. The dataset encompasses key financial metrics including billing amounts, insurance coverage, treatment costs, out-of-pocket expenses, and room charges, alongside clinical indicators such as length of stay, patient satisfaction scores, and diagnosis distributions.

The analysis was conducted using Microsoft Excel for data cleaning, transformation, and preliminary calculations, while Power BI was used for data modeling, DAX calculations, interactive dashboards, and visual analytics using data the from October 2024 – May 2025.
The dashboard enables healthcare administrators and decision-makers to monitor key performance indicators (KPIs), identify cost drivers, evaluate insurance contributions, and understand patient treatment trends across multiple cities, departments, diagnoses, and service types.

## Some DAX Measures & Calculations used

-- Total Billing Amount
Total Billing Amount = SUM(Healthcare[Billing Amount])

-- Total Treatment Cost
Total Treatment Cost = SUM(Healthcare[Treatment Cost])

-- Total Medication Cost
Total Medication Cost = SUM(Healthcare[Medication Cost])

-- Total Insurance Covered
Total Insurance Covered = SUM(Healthcare[Insurance Coverage])

-- Total Room Charges
Total Room Charges = SUM(Healthcare[Room Charges Daily Rate])

-- Out of Pocket
Out of Pocket = [Total Billing Amount] - [Total Insurance Covered]

-- Average Medication Cost
Avg Medication Cost = AVERAGE(Healthcare[Medication Cost])

-- Average Patient Satisfaction
Avg Patient Satisfaction = AVERAGE(Healthcare[Patient Satisfaction Score])

-- Average Treatment Cost
Avg Treatment Cost = AVERAGE(Healthcare[Treatment Cost])

-- Average Length of Stay
Avg Length of Stay = AVERAGE(Healthcare[Length of Stay])

-- Average Insurance Covered
Avg Insurance Covered = AVERAGE(Healthcare[Insurance Coverage])

-- Insurance Coverage % Male
Insurance Coverage Male % = 
DIVIDE(
    CALCULATE([Total Insurance Covered], Healthcare[Gender] = "Male"),
    [Total Insurance Covered]
)

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)


<img width="909" height="521" alt="Image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a7ae7926-cd37-40da-9431-da088b6be75d" />

The dashboard contains several KPI cards that provide an overview of healthcare performance.

### Total Billing Amount
This KPI represents the gross revenue generated by the healthcare facility before any deductions, adjustments, or insurance negotiations. It serves as the top-line financial metric, aggregating all charges from consultations, procedures, medications, room stays, and ancillary services. Tracking this over time helps identify revenue growth patterns, seasonal spikes, and the financial impact of new service lines. However, it must be analyzed alongside collection rates to assess true cash flow, as high billing doesn't always equate to high realized revenue of £3.356 Million.
### Total Treatment Cost
This KPI represents the total direct expenditure incurred on patient treatments, encompassing all clinical procedures, medications, staff time, and consumables across the organization. It serves as a critical cost-efficiency barometer, enabling administrators to track whether treatment spending aligns with patient volume, acuity, and revenue generation. Monitoring this metric over time helps identify cost inflation trends, evaluate the financial impact of new treatment protocols, and benchmark against industry standards. When analyzed alongside billing and insurance data, it reveals profitability margins per diagnosis or department, guiding strategic decisions on service line expansion, vendor negotiations, and operational streamlining. With all summing up to £2.63 Million.
### Total Medication Cost
This KPI captures the total pharmaceutical expenditure of £546,000 across all prescribed medications, serving as a critical metric for monitoring pharmacy spending patterns and identifying cost drivers. Tracking this value over time helps detect seasonal or diagnosis-driven medication trends, enabling proactive inventory management and bulk purchasing negotiations. 
### Total Insurance Covered
This KPI quantifies the £2.23 Million in payments received from insurance providers, reflecting the organization's reimbursement effectiveness and reliance on third-party payers for revenue collection. Tracking this value helps assess financial risk exposure, as higher insurance coverage reduces patient bad-debt risk but may indicate dependency on specific payer contracts. 
### Out-of-Pocket Cost
This KPI captures the £1.13 Million in direct payments made by patients, reflecting the financial burden placed on individuals after insurance deductions and adjustments. This value helps evaluate healthcare affordability across different demographics, diagnoses, and departments, while also identifying services with disproportionately high out-of-pocket expenses. It supports pricing strategy adjustments, financial counseling resource allocation, and policy decisions aimed at reducing patient financial distress and improving collection rates.
### Total Room Charges
This represents the £180,000 accumulated from inpatient room accommodation charges, serving as a key indicator of facility revenue generation from bed capacity utilization.  It supports capacity planning decisions, pricing adjustments for different room types (e.g., ICU vs. general ward), and operational improvements to maximize bed turnover without compromising patient care quality.

## Strategic Recommendations 
#### 1. Optimize Orthopedics & Cardiology Costs
There is a need to Conduct cost-benefit analyses, standardize treatment protocols, negotiate bulk purchasing, and introduce flexible payment plans to reduce out-of-pocket burden and improve collections.
#### 2. Enhance Patient Satisfaction for Chronic Conditions
There should be  quality initiatives for Hypertension, including patient education, remote monitoring, staff training in empathetic communication, and real-time feedback loops to raise satisfaction scores.
#### 3. Strengthen Insurance Reimbursement Strategies
Proper review of payer performance, renegotiate contracts with underperforming insurers, diversify payer mix, and adopt automated claims processing to accelerate reimbursements and reduce denials.
### 4. Expand Services in High-Demand Locations
Invest in new clinics and specialized services in top-performing cities like London and Manchester, while assessing underperforming markets for optimization.
